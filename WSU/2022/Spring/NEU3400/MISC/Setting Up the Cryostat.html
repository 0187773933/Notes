Setting Up the Cryostat

Cryostat Machine

Tools

  • Brushes

  • Sample Holders ( chucks ) :

    • Freeze the tissue onto the metal disk
    • Its what actually holds the sample in place while sectioning occurs

  • Put into box to "pre-chill"

  • Cryostat is kept at −20∘ C

  • Sectioning Device

    • Every time it goes through a cycle , it will advance the section holder a little bit to the front

  • Single Use Cryostat Blades

    • 1 Edge Razor Blades

  • Optimal Cutting Temperature Compound ( O.C.T. )

    • Used to mount tissue onto the chuck
    • Starts out as clear liquid , but when it freezes , it turns opaque = "dark , shaded , unlit" = "impervious to the rays of light"

  • Put on top of tissue sample , which is on top of chuck :

  • Turns white as it freezes :

  • Slides :

  • Roll Guard :

    • prevents the tissue samples from "rolling up" as you are cutting them
    • take in and out every time you use the cyrostat :

Setup Sequence

  1. Check positioning of sample holder

    • should be set to around 11∘

    • each mark = 2∘

  2. Blade Holder Lock - Forward / Back

    • You want to bring the whole apparatus close to where the tissue is on the sectioning device

  3. Orange Lock = allows for pivoting of sectioning device

    • Normally , you don't need to mess with this

  4. Open "plate" so you can insert the Roll Guard ( glass piece )

    • Make sure grooves are facing down

      • so that as the tissue comes underneath the roll plate , it has clearance space

    • this step should be done early in the whole setup sequence , because the roll plate starts at room temperature , and needs time to cool down

  5. Verify tissue sample is frozen ( O.C.T should be solid white )

  6. Mount tissue sample into sectioning device :

  7. Tighten Pivot Knob to keep sample from rotating

  8. Get a blade out of the blade dispenser

    • Pull black slider piece with arrow all the way back

    • Then push black arrow piece slowly forward , should expose a single blade

  9. Attach blade to cryostat

    • there is a tiny groove that it drops into

    • make sure it is centered ( left and right )

    • make sure it is not off at a weird angle ( should be flush with roll guard plate )

  10. Use right-side orange locking handle to clamp the blade into place
