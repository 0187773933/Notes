Integumentary System

Protection

  • Protects underlying structures from abrasion

  • Protection from pathogens and toxic substances

    • Loss of skin tissue ( eg , with third degree burns ) can lead to dangerous infections

  • Prevents loss of Interstitial fluid

    • Loss of skin tissue ( eg , with third degree burns ) can lead to interstitial fluid loss

      • Interstitial fluid loss would lead to loss of fluids from other compartments

        • eg , loss of fluid from the blood , which would lower blood pressure

  • Protection from ultraviolet rays ( both UVA and UVB )

  • Melanin

    • Absorbs ultraviolet ( UV ) rays

      • UV rays are ionizing radiation

        • Can damage DNA , protein , membranes , etc

      • UV rays can cause oxidation via free radical production

        • Free radicals damage DNA , proteins , membranes , etc

    • Production of melanin by melanocytes

      • "melanin sponges up UV rays"

      • Melanocytes scattered within the stratum basale

        • Melanocytes make up about 10% of stratum basale layer

          • Everyone has the same number of melanocytes

      • Melanocytes contain the enzyme tyrosinase

        • Converts tyrosine into dopamine

          • Dopamine converted to melanin in subsequent steps

            • Melanin stored in melanosomes
        Tyrosine →UVBTyrosinase Dopamine⟶Melanin

        • Basal activity of tyrosinase dictates natural skin color

          • Dark-skinned people have a higher activity of tyrosinase
          • Light-skinned people have a lower activity of tyrosinase

        • Tyrosinase stimulated by UVA and UVB ( mainly UVB )

          • Reason people get darker skin with sun exposure

    • Albinism

      • Genetic condition in which tyrosinase is NOT produced

        • Melanocytes cannot produce melanin

    • Vitiligo

      • Autoimmune disease that destroys melanocytes

        • Results in irregular , pale patches of skin ( "hyperpigmented" )

  • UV Rays and the Dermis

    • Dermis is composed mainly of collagen and elastin fibers

      • Impart mechanical strength , flexibility , and elasticity
      • Loss or damage of collagen and elastin fibers causes wrinkles

    • UVA ( 320 to 400 nm ) penetrates into the dermal layer

      • Penetrates deeper than UVB

        • Therefore doesn't stimulate tyrosinase as much

      • Free radical production damages collagen and elastin fibers

        • Overexposure to UVA is one of the causes of wrinkles

          • Wrinkles also caused by aging

    • UVB ( 280 to 320 nm ) can NOT penetrate into the dermal layer

      • UVB is still potentially damaging to the skin

    • There is no scale that represents how well a lotion protects against UVA

      • However lotions do protect against UVA

Production of Vitamin D

  • Initial step occurs in stratum basale cells

    • 7-dehydrocholesterol converted to cholecalciferol ( Vitamin D3 )

      • Occurs upon exposure to UVB ( catalyzes )

        • Approximately 60 minutes of UVB exposure per week is adequate to make sufficient amount of Vitamin D3

          • Longer time needed for dark-skinned people
          • Shorter time needed for light-skinned people

  • Subsequent steps occur in the liver followed by the kidneys

    • Cholecalciferol circulates to the liver

      • Converted to calcidiol ( 25-hydroxyvitamin D ) ( Vitamin D3 ) and stored

    • Calcidiol circulates to the kidneys

      • Converted to Calcitriol ( 1,25-dihydroxycholecalciferol ) ( Vitamin D3 )

        • Most biologically active form of Vitamin D3

Temperature Regulation

  • Via blood vessels within the dermal layer ( epidermis does not contain blood vessels )

    • With an increase in body temperature

      • Blood vessels dilate

        • Blood ( which carries heat ) is diverted towards surface of the skin

          • Heat loss from blood ( ie , body ) is increased

            • Body temperature lowered towards normal range

    • With a decrease in body temperature

      • Blood vessels constrict

        • Blood ( which carries heat ) is diverted away from surface of the skin

          • Heat loss from blood ( ie , body ) is minimized

            • Body temperature increased towards normal range

  • Via Sweat Glands

    • Sweat is continuously produced ( ~ one quart each day ) and secreted onto the skin

      • Evaporation of sweat helps cool the body to maintain body temperature

        • Body is continually cooled due to heat generated by metabolism

    • An increase in body temperature increases the production and secretion of sweat

Excretion of Nitrogenous Waste

  • Via Sweat Glands

    • Sweat contains a small amount of ammonia and urea ( both are nitrogenous waste )

    • Other constituents of sweat

      • Water

      • Ions ( primarily Na+,K+,Ca2+,Mg2+, and Cl− )

        • High levels of Na+ and Cl− ( mainly Cl− ) can indicate cystic fibrosis

          • Pilocarpine sweat test used to measure Cl− concentrations

        • Cystic fibrosis is diagnosed by testing the level of Cl− in the sweat

          • If chloride is 5 times what is normally should be , then the patient has cystic fibrosis

Sense Organ ( via sensory receptors )

  • Hair = responds to light pressure ( hair follicle contains sensory receptors )
  • Merkel's Disk = responds to light pressure
  • Meissner's Corpuscle = responds to light pressure and vibration
  • Pacinian Corpuscle = responds to deep pressure ( overrides light touch signals )
  • Ruffini's Corpuscle = responds to skin stretch
  • Free Nerve Ending = responds to pain , temperature , itch , and titckle

Burns

  • One of the most serious and frequent problems that affect skin

  • Rule of nines ( adults , different for children )

    • Head = 9%

    • Front of torso = 18%

    • Back of torso = 18%

    • Entire leg = 18%

      • Front or back of leg = 9%

    • Entire arm = 9%

      • Front or back of arm = 4.5%

    • Genitalia = 1%

  • Severity of Burns

    • First Degree

      • midle sunburn

      • Partial thickness burn ( only part of the skin is affected )

      • Only epidermis is injured

        • Will NOT blister

      • Inflammation : blood vessels dilate increasing blood flow ( skin appears red )

        • Attempts to dissipate heat and deliver nutrients for healing

      • Will heal naturally

    • Second Degree

      • Partial thickness burn

      • 100% Epidermis and part of the dermis are injured

        • Often ( but not always ) will blister

          • Depends on how deep

      • Inflammation = blood vessels dialate

        • increase in blood flow to area inflamed
        • brings oxygen and nutrients for healing

      • Normally will heal naturally

    • Third Degree

      • all of skin is gone

      • full thickness burn

      • Epidermis and all of the dermis are injured

      • Fluid floss and infection can become life threatening

      • Will NOT heal natrually

        • Skin Graft

          • Autograft = skin from unburned region of affected person
          • Homograft = skin from a donor

        • Artificial skin ( not permanent )

Various Skin Conditions

  • Psoriasis

    • Inflammation marked by red lesions with silvery scaling
    • Pruritus ( itching ) often present
    • Autoimmune damage to skin
    • Not contagious
    • Treatment = immunosuppressant medications , anti-inflammatory creams

  • Dermatitis ( eg , eczema , poison ivy )

    • Inflammation marked by red lesions

    • Pruritus often present

    • Caused by increased sensitivity or allergic reaction to specific triggers

      • Takes hours to days to start and can be present for days to weeks

    • Not contagious

      • Only poison ivy oil is contagious

    • Treatment = Anti-inflammatory medications and creams , antihistamine medications

  • Urticaria / Hives

    • Inflammation of blood vessels under the skin

    • Pruritus often present

    • Caused by increased sensitivity or allergic reaction to specific triggers

      • Takes minutes to start and is present for just minutes to hours

    • Not contagious

    • Treatment = typically resolves on its own

  • Dermatophytosis

    • Fungal , not a worm

    • Affects all parts of body

      • Ringworm
      • Athlete's foot

    • Caused by fungus

    • Contagious = spread by direct and indirect contact

    • Treatment = antifungal medications , antifungal creams

  • Impetigo

    • bacterial

    • Lesions that typically form around the mouth , nose or neck

      • Can form on other parts of the body

    • Caused by bacteria

    • Contagious = spread by direct and indirect contact

    • Treatment = antibiotic medications , antibiotic creams

    • Seen in kids more than adults

    • Prefers the mouth

  • Warts

    • = skin cells dividing uncontrollably , just not "cancerous"
    • viral
    • Elevated skin lesion due to abnormal skin cell division
    • Caused by the human papillomavirus ( HPV )
    • Contagious = spread by direct and indirect contact
    • Treatment = "burn it off" aka acid , salicylic acid , cryotherapy , surgery with plantar warts

  • Shingles / Herpes Zoster

    • viral

    • Red , swollen vesicles in a single dermatome that rupture and crust

    • Caused by the varicella zoster virus of chickenpox

      • Virus lies dormant in nerves but can shed , causing shingles

    • Compromised immune system , stress , and aging are leading factors

      • Allow the virus to shed from nerves

    • Contagious = spread by direct and indirect contact

      • Would cause chickenpox AND NOT shingles ( cannot "catch" shingles )

      • You can't get shingles if you've never had chickenpox

        • Because chickenpox never technically goes away

      • You can't catch shingles , but you can catch chickenpox.

        • Chickenpox leads to shingles

    • Treatment = anti-viral medications , anti-inflammatory medications

  • Herpes

    • Pustulous blistering of the skin

    • Caused by herpes simplex virus ( HSV )

    • Studies show that approximately 75% of adults have oral herpes ( HSV-1 )

      • Most that have it do not exhibit signs and/or symptoms ( ie , are unaware )

    • Studies show that approximately 30% of adults have genital herpes ( HSV-2 )

      • Most that have it periodically exhibit signs and/or symptoms

    • Contagious = spread by direct contact ( HSV-1 and HSV-2 ) and indirect contact ( HSV-1 )

    • Treatment = anti-viral medications , lysine ( starves herpes virus )

    • Can you get genital herpes from using a pubic bathroom?

      • NO !!! Only through direct contact

Various Nail Conditions

  • Brittle nails = can be due to cold , dry conditions or overexposure to water or thyroid disease
  • Yellow nails = can be due to dermatophytosis or pulmonary disease or diabetes
  • White spots = most often due to trauma to nail body or could be indication of zinc deficiency
  • Pitting = can be due to psoriasis beneath the nail body
  • Concave = can be due to iron deficiency ( can take some time to manifest itself )
  • Pale = can be due to aging , anemia , or cardiovascular disease or liver disease
  • Dark Vertical Lines = can be due to trauma ( causing bruising ) or melanoma
