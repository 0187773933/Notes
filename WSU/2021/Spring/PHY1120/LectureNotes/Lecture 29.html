Sinusoidal Waves

  • Waves can have any "shape" , but we often use sinusoids

    • Nature often does this
    • Any wave, even strange shapes, can be broken down into sum of sinusoids, each with a different frequency ( superposition )

  • We've been sloppy with units for waves

  • We've written stuff like y(t)=3∗m∗cos(2∗π∗(x−(5∗t)))

    • But argument of sine or cosine always must be dimensionless
    • So in above expression, x must be multiplied by something with units of 1length
    • And t must be multiplied by something with units of 1time

  • So let's write it as y(t)=A∗cos((k∗x)−(ω∗t))

    • ω is our familiar angular frequency with dimensions of radianssecond or just 1second

  • This k , is completely new

    • It's not a spring constant or any other k we've used
    • It's called "wave number"
    • Dimensions = 1length

  • Example, earlier expression: y(t)=3∗m∗cos(2∗π∗(x−(5∗t)))

    • k = coefficient in front of x→12∗π meters

      • k=2∗πWavelength (λ)

    • ω = coefficient in front of t→12∗π∗5=110∗π seconds

    • Don't worry about signs.

      • Signs determine direction wave is moving, but you're pretty safe letting k and ω always be positive

  • Wave speed = speed that the crest is moving

    • Remember, not transverse speed of mass
    • Wave Velocity (v)=Angular Frequency (ω)Wave Number (k)

  • So if you have equation, you can find wave speed by looking at coefficients of x and t

    • Example, our wave: y(t)=3∗m∗cos(2∗π∗(x−(5∗t)))

      • ω=10∗π
      • k=2∗π
      • v=10∗π2∗π=5 meters1 second

     

Beats

  • 2 waves, close in frequency

  • When they are "in sync"

    • the amplitude is doubled
    • Crest adds to Crest

  • When they are "out of sync"

    • Crests adds to Trough

    • You get zero amplitude

      • Aka, no sound

  • The frequency that they go "in and out of sync" depends on difference in 2 original frequencies

 

Wavelength

  • λ = distance between crests

    • or length ( in distance ) of 1 full wave

  • also, k=2∗πλ

    • or λ=2∗πk
