Line Charge , Electric Field

  • Concept of linear charge density
λ=CoulombMeter

  • For example if λ=3 Coulombs1 meter on a long cylinder ( a rod ) ( uniformly spread )

    • If I have a length of 1 meter of this rod, the total charge on the rod is Q = 3 Coulombs
    • If I have a length of 4 meters, the total charge on the rod is Q=λ∗4=12 Coulombs

    =3 Coulombs1 meter∗4 meters=12 Coulombs
    =12 Coulombs4 meters=3 Coulombs1 meter

     

    • If the length of the subject is ∞ , I have Q=∞

      • But I still have λ=3 Coulombs1 meter = a linear charge density

  • We will soon also have an area of charge density which has units of Coulombsmeters2

 

  • Electric Field

    • Direction

      • Directed straight away in all directions, so it's 3-dimensional ( like bottle brush )

      • Readings discussed why this is true.

        • Its due to cancellation of any Electric Field that might point to the left or right

 

  • Magnitude

    • Gets weaker as you get farther away
    • Specifically:
    |E⇀|=2∗kc∗λd
    • d is (perpendicular) distance from the line
    • |E⇀| increases if λ increases
    • |E⇀| decreases if d increases

     

    • Compare this equation to that for a point charge:
    |E⇀|=kc∗qr2

    • Notice that point charge falls of as r2 , but line charge only falls off as d ( no squared in denominator).

      • So that E field from a point charge gets smaller faster
