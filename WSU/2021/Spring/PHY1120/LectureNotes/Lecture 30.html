Standing Waves

  • Mathematically - derived by adding 2 waves going in opposite directions

    • Not super important to see that math

  • More important is that they represent how a string oscillates when both ends are fixed

    • (or 1 end is fixed and the other is completely free)

  • Although called standing waves , the waves don't go anywhere

    • They oscillate in place

  • An infinite number of "normal modes" are possible:

  • In music, these are called "harmonics" or "overtones"

  • Each normal mode oscillates at a different frequency

  • Mathematics:

    • L is length of string, fixed at both ends

    • Find λ from stretch , express in terms of fraction of L

    • v0 for wave fixed , still Wave Velocity (v0)=Tμ

    • So v0=ωk also , and

      • ω=2∗π∗f
      • k=2∗piλ
      • v0=λ∗f
      • f=v0λ

    • Example: for n=3 ( 3rd normal mode ) :

      • λ=2∗L3
      • f=v02∗L3=3∗v02∗L
