2nd Law

  • Direction things chagne is so entropy of the total system increases

    • Total system = either the whole universe, or else an isolated system

  • Q = change in heat energy of an object

    • Its really a ΔQ , but by convention we don't use a Δ in front
    • If heat energy of an object goes up, Q is positive
    • If heat energy of an object goes down, Q is negative
Δ=Final−Initial
ΔS=change in entropy=QT
S=kB∗ln(Number of Microstates (W))
  • For a hot object near a cold object, Q moves from hot to cold object:
ΔSH=−QTH;so entropy decreases (it loses heat)
ΔSC=+QTC;so entropy increases (it gains heat)
  • But because TC is smaller than TH , the entropy gained by cold object is greater than entropy lost by hot object, so net entropy of the system goes up.
