Dielectric Constant

  • When matter is exposed to charge, we know that the charge "pulls" opposite charge in matter towards itself

  • Electric field is created by charges on plates:

  • Put material between plates:

  • E due only to rearrangement of charges inside the material is to the left ( points from positive to negative )
  • So E from charges on plates is to the right, and E from charges inside the material is to the left
Enet inside material=Ebetween plates if vacuum−Edue to material charges

  • The net E inside the material is weaker (smaller) than it would be without the material

    • The material has "covered up" some of the E field that would have been there if there were a vacuum between the plates

  • We say the material has been polarized by the E field.

    • The material itself now has a "left" side and a "right" side ( with negative and positive charges ).

      • It has "poles"

  • How much is E reduced?

    • Depends on the material:
Enet in material=Ebetween plates if vaccumk

  • k is called the "dielectric constant".

    • Always greater than or equal to 1

  • Also changes capacitance.

    • C becomes:
    C=k∗A4∗π∗kc∗d
    • So Capacitance (C) increases due to material
    • For almost all real capacitors, material is present between plates

 

Conductors

  • Metals

  • Charges are very free to move in a donductor

    • Not bound to nucleus

  • If you put a conductor between the plates of a capacitor, the charges exactly match the outer plates:

  • So E from plates is to the right, and E due to charges on the conductor is to the left
  • Because charges exactly match, the net E inside the conductor = 0
  • In fact, in all charge situations (with nothing moving) , the electric field inside a conductor is zero
  • So if E exists inside a conductor, free charges will feel force and move
