Ray Model

  • Ordinary surfaces:

    • People at many locations can "see" the wall by seeing light coming off the wall
    • They can also see the light bulb if they look directly at the light bulb
    • However, they can't see the light bulb by looking at the wall

  • Mirrors / Reflective Surface

    • Light from bulb hitting mirror at 1 spot only "bounces" in 1 direction
    • If you're standing in one location , you can only see the light bulb's reflection by looking at one specific spot on mirror
    • You can "see" the light bulb in the mirror
    • But if the room were dark, you couldn't see the rest of the mirror

    • Refraction (light ray going from 1 clear medium (like air) to a different clear medium (like water))

      • stands for incident (incoming) light

      • stands for transmitted light

      • = index of refraction for air

      • = index of refraction for water

      • Rule:

        • This is "Snell's Law"
